# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

Research-Wiki is a research infrastructure project. We expect every
participant to help keep the project rigorous, respectful, and welcoming.

We pledge to make participation in this project harassment-free for
people of any age, body size, caste, disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- Focus criticism on ideas, code, documentation, and protocol design, not
  on people.
- Preserve the project's research-boundary invariants when proposing
  changes.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, or demeaning language.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional research or open-source setting.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of
acceptable behavior and may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits,
issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not align with
this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for
behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the project
maintainers through the repository issue tracker or the private contact
channel listed by the maintainers. Reports will be handled with care and
confidentiality to the extent possible.
